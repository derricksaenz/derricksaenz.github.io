Apply For Graduation Poster

While this poster was a relatively simple project, it was still a very interesting endeavor to partake in. This task was given to me by my office job, one that doesn’t require graphic design experience whatsoever. My task was to make a flyer to inform students about the graduation process. After making an initial word document with this same exact information, I had some time on my hands and decided to make a larger poster to add to screens around the art building and to send in an email out to students instead of the regular boring Word Document.

This was the result, one that I think adheres to the new branding San Jose State University uses across a lot of its new media. As a student assistant in the Art Office, I was given access to the SJSU Spartan font as well as a library of student submitted photos, and from there, I was able to make a poster that is both visually appealing and is able to look the part as an official poster from the university.

This poster also serves as an example in how I work with text both in the writing and in the typography. In writing, while I had ideas I needed to hit, most of the text is there to ensure that I delivered clear and concise points to the reader. In the typography, I made sure to create a clear sense of hierarchy in the message. While the text at the top is aligned right, usually not recommended for the bodycopy, I used it to reinforce the asymmetry of the section. The QR Code in the bottom right corner creates a weight that already promotes an asymmetrical look, so I wanted to make sure the weight wasn’t alone in its “push” to the right side of the page. Above that, it is a short read, so the reader won’t get lost in the short section.
